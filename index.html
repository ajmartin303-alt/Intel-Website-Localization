Intel Sustainability Timeline

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename the newly formed company NM Electronics to Intel Corporation.

Intel begins its journey toward becoming a global technology leader.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor.

The 4004 launched the microprocessor era.

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture.

x86 remains one of the most influential computing standards.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture.

32-bit computing increased power and efficiency.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel reaches its highest annual greenhouse gas emissions level.

Sustainability efforts intensified afterward.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE strategy and ambitious 2030 goals.

The strategy established measurable sustainability targets.

2022

Net-Zero by 2040

Intel commits to achieving net-zero greenhouse gas emissions.

Building on years of environmental initiatives.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide.

Renewable energy drives sustainability success.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit.

Industry leaders collaborate on a sustainable future.
