Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename the newly formed company NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of technological innovation.

Intel begins its journey toward becoming a global technology leader.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the microprocessor revolution and propelling the future of computing devices.

The 4004 changed computing forever and launched the microprocessor era.

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture that drives countless PCs and servers in the modern era.

The x86 architecture remains one of the most influential computing standards.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture, ushering in a new era of performance and multitasking for personal computers.

32-bit computing significantly increased power and efficiency.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

This year marks Intel's highest annual greenhouse gas emissions for operations.

Intel began aggressively reducing emissions through sustainability initiatives.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE strategy and 2030 goals.

The RISE strategy established measurable sustainability goals.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel commits to achieving net-zero greenhouse gas emissions.

Building on years of environmental initiatives.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide.

Renewable energy becomes a major contributor to sustainability success.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit.

Industry leaders collaborate on a more sustainable future.
